User Guide & Costing Principles
Core Costing Principles in BakeFlow

BakeFlow uses a **Direct Costing Model** where only the direct, incremental expenses involved in creating a specific product are counted inside the Cost Calculator. This ensures your margins are accurate and scalable.

📈 Direct COGS (Billed to Recipes)
  • Raw Materials (Flour, butter, sugar, etc.)
  • Packaging (Boxes, board, carry bag, tags)
  • Decorations (Fondant, toppers, candles)
  • Direct Production Labour (Staff active time)
🏢 Operating Expenses (Deducted from GP)
  • Rent & Society Maintenance
  • Utilities (Electricity, Water, LPG/Gas, Fuel)
  • Business Admin (Licenses, Software, Professional fees)
  • Non-Production Staff (Sales, cashiers, delivery, managers)
Simple vs. Advanced Labor Costing

Labour costing can be calculated using either the **Simple (Effort-Based)** or the **Advanced (Active Time-Based)** method. You can switch between them using the dropdown inside the Cost Calculator.

Feature Simple (Effort Points) Method Advanced (Active Time) Method
Core Metric Complexity multipliers (1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 3.0 pts) Active minutes + direct staff count
Best Used For Quick pricing, standard cupcakes/brownies, catalog cakes. Highly complex custom fondant or tiered theme cakes.
Math Formula Effort Points × Cost Per Point × Batch Size (Active Minutes / 60) × Derived Hourly Rate
Why Use It? Saves time by grouping recipes into difficulty levels. Captures precise human hours for labor-intensive details.
Step-by-Step Use Cases
Case A: Standard Cookie Box (Simple Method)

1. Open **Cost Calculator**. Add raw materials (chocolate, flour, butter) and packaging box.
2. Choose **Simple Method** in the labor section.
3. Rate the difficulty (e.g. standard batch = `1.0 Effort Point`). Labor cost is auto-calculated using the team's capacity.
4. Adjust margin slider to 60%, save to Catalog as a standard product.

Case B: 3-Tier Custom Wedding Cake (Advanced Method)

1. Open **Cost Calculator**, add rich ingredients, packaging, and specific toppers/decorations.
2. Select **Advanced Method** in labor costing.
3. Set Batch Active Time (e.g., 90 mins for baking/assembly with 2 staff members) and Unit Active Time (e.g., 180 mins decoration for icing detail with 1 decorator).
4. The system calculates the total direct labor hours, computes labor cost using the team's salary-derived hourly rate, and adds it to the COGS subtotal.

Essential BakeFlow Tips
  • Wastage Buffer: Always set a 3-5% wastage/buffer percentage in the calculator for raw material spillages and scrap loss.
  • Inventory Depletion: When you record a Sales Invoice, the system will search for a recipe matching the product name and automatically deduct the ingredients and packaging from your inventory.
  • Double-counting: Do not count occupancy (rent) or cleaning supplies as ingredients or custom overheads in the calculator; they are already handled globally as monthly operating expenses.
